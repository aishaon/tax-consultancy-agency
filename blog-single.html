Understanding Income Tax in Bangladesh: A Complete Guide for 2024-2025

Ajay & Safiullah Associates June 15, 2026 Tax

Income tax is a crucial aspect of financial planning for both individuals and businesses in Bangladesh. Understanding the tax system, rates, filing procedures, and available deductions can help you optimize your tax position and ensure compliance with the National Board of Revenue (NBR) regulations.

What is Income Tax in Bangladesh?

Income tax is a direct tax levied by the government on the income of individuals, businesses, and other entities. In Bangladesh, the National Board of Revenue (NBR) under the Internal Resources Division (IRD) of the Ministry of Finance administers income tax laws. The tax system is governed by the Income Tax Ordinance, 1984, and the Income Tax Rules, 1984.

Who Needs to File Income Tax Returns?

The following individuals and entities are required to file income tax returns in Bangladesh:

  • Individuals whose total income exceeds the tax-free threshold
  • All companies registered in Bangladesh
  • Partnership firms and proprietorships meeting certain criteria
  • Non-resident individuals with income from Bangladesh
  • Individuals owning specific assets (e.g., motor vehicles, multiple properties)

Income Tax Rates for 2024-2025

For individual taxpayers (male), the tax-free income limit is BDT 3,50,000. The tax rates are structured as follows:

Income Slab (BDT) Tax Rate
First 3,50,0000% (Tax-free)
Next 1,00,000 (3,50,001 - 4,50,000)5%
Next 3,00,000 (4,50,001 - 7,50,000)10%
Next 4,00,000 (7,50,001 - 11,50,000)15%
Next 5,00,000 (11,50,001 - 16,50,000)20%
Above 16,50,00025%

Key Deductions and Allowances

Taxpayers can reduce their tax liability through various deductions and allowances under different sections of the Income Tax Ordinance:

  • Investment allowance (Section 44): Up to 15% of total income or BDT 15,00,000 (whichever is less) for investments in specified instruments like savings certificates, shares, and life insurance premiums.
  • House rent allowance: Lower of actual rent paid less 50% of basic salary, 25% of basic salary, or BDT 30,000 per month.
  • Medical allowance: Actual medical expenses up to 10% of basic salary.
  • Contributions to provident fund: Employee's contribution to recognized provident funds.
